Strollable ("we", "our", or "us") is operated as a sole proprietor based in British Columbia, Canada. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights under British Columbia's Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA) and Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA).

By using Strollable, you agree to the collection and use of information as described in this policy.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account Information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Email address — used for authentication and account communication
  • Display name / username — auto-generated; shown publicly on your profile and on location submissions you contribute
  • Sign-in method — Google OAuth, Apple Sign-In, or email magic link

1.2 Location Data

  • Approximate GPS coordinates — collected in-app only when you use the Near Me button or Emergency Mode (nearest change table). We do not store your GPS history. Location is processed on-device and used only for the current session feature.
  • Submitted location data — addresses and coordinates you voluntarily submit as part of a location contribution are stored in our database and displayed on the map.

1.3 Content You Submit

  • Location names, descriptions, categories, amenity details, hours, phone numbers, and websites you submit
  • Photos you upload (stored in Supabase Storage)
  • Issue reports you file on existing locations
  • Your saved places list

1.4 Usage Data

  • Analytics events collected via PostHog: feature interactions (e.g., filter_applied, emergency_mode_activated, location_submitted). Events are associated with a randomly-assigned user ID, not your email. We use this to understand which features are useful and to improve the app.
  • Crash reports collected via Sentry: device type, OS version, app version, and stack traces when the app crashes. No personally identifying information is included in crash reports.

1.5 Device Information

We do not collect device identifiers (IMEI, advertising IDs) beyond what PostHog and Sentry collect automatically for session tracking.

3. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored in Supabase (Supabase Inc., servers in AWS us-east-1). Supabase is SOC 2 Type II certified.

Analytics data is stored in PostHog (PostHog Inc., EU cloud region).

Crash data is stored in Sentry (Functional Software Inc., servers in US).

We use row-level security (RLS) so each user can only read and modify their own account data. All connections use TLS encryption in transit. Passwords are never stored — we use email magic links and OAuth only.

4. Data Retention

  • Account data: retained while your account is active. When you delete your account, your profile, saved places, and badges are permanently deleted within 24 hours. Location submissions you made remain on the map as community contributions — your username is removed from them.
  • Analytics events: retained for 12 months in PostHog, then automatically purged.
  • Crash reports: retained for 90 days in Sentry.
  • Photos: deleted from Supabase Storage when the associated location is removed.

6. Children's Privacy

Strollable is designed for parents and caregivers aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has created an account, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

7. Your Rights

Under PIPA (BC) and PIPEDA, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Delete your account and associated personal data (via Settings → Delete Account, or by emailing us)
  • Withdraw consent at any time, which may mean we can no longer provide the service
  • Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner for BC

To exercise any of these rights, email us at strollable.vancouver@gmail.com.

8. Cookies and Tracking

The Strollable mobile app does not use browser cookies. PostHog uses a persistent anonymous identifier stored in device storage to connect sessions from the same user; this is not linked to your email or name.
